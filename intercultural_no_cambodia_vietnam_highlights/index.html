Intercultural threads

Stone chariots, calligraphed minarets and marble gardens tell stories of trade winds and shared beliefs. From India, ideas and artisans travelled along sea routes, shaping temple towers and mythic carvings across Southeast Asia. At the same time, influences from Central Asia, Persia and Europe entered the subcontinent and can still be read in arches, domes and city plans.

About Our School


Introduction


M. C. Kejriwal Vidyapeeth (MCKV) is an English medium institution affiliated with the Council for the Indian School Certificate Examinations, dedicated to nurturing students for success in the ICSE (Class 10) and ISC (Class 12) examinations. Since its establishment on 15 June 1997, the school has made significant strides in both academic achievements and co-curricular endeavours.

Originally founded exclusively for boys, MCKV embraced a pivotal change in April 2024, transitioning to a co-educational model by welcoming girls into the pre-primary section. This transition will be gradual, extending to encompass all classes. This shift underscores the school's commitment to inclusivity and responsiveness to evolving educational paradigms.

The curriculum framework derives its inspiration from the National Education Policy 2020, offering a multi-dimensional approach that is outcome-specific and geared towards fostering 21st-century skills such as critical thinking, effective communication, creativity, and cross-cultural understanding. Central to its ethos is a student-centric approach, fostering care and recognizing the unique creative talents, interests, and inclinations of each individual. As a result, its students emerge as thoughtful, self-assured individuals, well-equipped to navigate life's complexities.

Recognition of an International Dimension in Schools

The India RIDS programme, formerly known as ISA and then IDS, rewards schools committed to embedding international awareness within their curriculum.


RIDS provides a framework for schools to develop international activities across the curriculum, including collaborative work with partner schools, and opportunities to involve the wider community. Schools plan, execute and evaluate a range of project-based school activities which are then assessed against the RIDS criteria for certification.


The India RIDS programme has evolved from the British Council’s global International School Award (ISA) programme which was active in 31 countries worldwide and is currently being delivered in the vUK, Greece, and Nigeria. In India, ISA/IDS ran from 2003 to 2020, reaching over 4000 private and government schools.
